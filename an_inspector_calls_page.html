Characters

Mr Birling - The Spirit of Capitalism

Mr Birling is seen as a heavy-looking man in his middle fifties who is colloquial in how he speaks. He can be seen as very proud and strong willed which is shown by how much he brings up his achivements and how he has worked his way up. Since he was not born into the upper class lifestyle he does not know how to socially behave in the environment he is in which is shown when he praises the cook and is reprimanded by Mrs Birling. Howver because of this he is aware of his social superiority and shows how selfish he as has he has no care for community and in some instances family "A Man has to make his own way" This is also shown by his dedication to protecting his own Reputation instead of caring about Eva's death

Mrs Birling - The Spirit of Capitalism

Mrs Birling is a woman in her fifties who is seen as cold and superior to her husband. She acts stubborn and pretenttious because she is very aware of her social class and will think little of people who are of a lower class Out of all oif the character she shows the least respect for the Inspector which is shown when she tries to intimidate him into leaving and lies to him. She is also denies alot of facts that she does not want to belive such as Eric's drinking and Gerald's affair making her manipulative in distress. She also has a narow sense of morality which is shown by her prejudice against Eva and her belief that she did nothing wrong.

Sheila Birling - Spirit of the New Generation

Shiela is a woman in her early twenties. She can be shown to be very naive and shallow which is shown by her suspicious nature with Gerald. Howvever unlike her parents Shiela shows compassion to the treatment of Eva and she realises her mistakes and decides to change. She also shows herself to be perceptive and curious to the events that happen throughout the story and is shown to grow throughout the story becoming wiser to the event that go on around her and is one of the characters to change.

Eric Birling - Spirit of the New Generation

Eric is a male in his early twenties who is quite shy. He is also shown to be embarassed and awkward about something throughout the story which is discovered is the stolen money. He is shown to have a very strong sense of responsibity which is shown by how he admits his guilt and scolds his parents for not admiting their mistakes. This is also reinforced by how he supports workers causes and his frustrations over everything that has happened in the past.

Gerald Croft - The Middle Ground

Gerald Croft is an Man in his thirties who is in the middle and isnt a dandy man but who isnt particularly manly either much like the rest of the characters involved he is an aristocrat and is the son of a Lord and a Lady. He is shown to not be willing to admit his part in Eva's death and would rather protect his own interests which is shown by how he doesnt change after he thinks he is "off the hook" and does not learn anything from the experience and how he tries to gather as much evidence to prove the inspector does not exist in act 3. However unlike Mr and Mrs Birling he is more on both sides as he is shown to have genuine feelings for Eva and is torn in the middle.

Inspector Goole - Spirit of Socialism

"An impression of massiveness,solidify and purposefulness" This is how Inspector Goole is described and perfectly depicts his presence on the story and the characters. Inspecto Goole is a man in his fifties who seems to be an omnipinant figure who knows all about the mistakes that the family has made over the years and as such use them to punish them. There are many questions around Goole such as if he is a real person or if he is the voice of Priestly the author.

Themes

Theme 1- Social Message

The first theme that is greatly used in the novel is the social message that Priestly is trying to convey this being the drastic difference between social classes and how they are treated. This can be seen by how Mr and Mrs Birling talk, this is how Priestly wanted to show the clear divide between people and classes

Theme 2- Responsibility

The theme of responsibility is important in the novel as it is the main purpose for the troubles that the characters feel, this theme is also what splits the family in two showing the difference in the time. This theme also supports the theme of social status as the older generation refuses to change and hold resposibility for the actions while the younger generation thats is less bound by social status are willing to change and see there mistakes.

Theme 3- Morality

As well as responsibility the theme of morality plays are large part in the novels and its what makes the characters act this can be seen with the different characters, Mr and Mrs Birling seem to have a lack of morality and do not regret the actions that they have taken while Shiela and Eric want to change this moral compass also helps to expand the other two themes as they all link together.

Quotes

  • "A man has to make his own way" - Said by Mr Birling and shows how he is selfish and only seems to care about himself.
  • "Girls of that Class" - Said by Mrs Birling and shows her snobbish attitude and how she does not respect people of lower class than herself.
  • "Last Summer when you never came near me" - Said by Shiela Birling and shows her Naive and Shallow nature.
  • "But these girls aren't cheap labour they're people" - Said by Shiela Birling and shows her compassion when she hears about her fathers treatment of the working girls.
  • "Why shouldn't they try for higher wages?" - Said by Eric Birling and shows how he supports the workers and what they want to do.
