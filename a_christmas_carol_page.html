Characters

Ebenezer Scrooge - Owner of a London country house

Scrooge is a Miserable man who can easily be described as Cold Hearted, Ill Mannered, Tight-fisted, Miserly and Self Deluded this is to make him disliked by the reader and makes his change of heart after going through the events of the story more interesting as he starts as a moody person with no respect for anyone into someone who has clearly changed and is looking for forgiveness.

Bob Cratchit - Scrooge's Clerk

Bob is a Humble and Hard working man who works for Scrooge his is shown to be kind and dedicated especially in order to provide for his family this is shown by how he works in unpleasant conditions without any complaint this also shows his obediance. Bob is also show to be generous as he raises a toastto Scrooge despite what he has to go through because of him and finnaly he is show to be sensitive due to the love of son that is shown through his grief because of Tiny Tim's Death

The Ghost of Chirstmas Past

The Ghost of Christmas Past is described as young and old with a commanding presence tat is streaming with light this is shown by how scrooge attempts to extinguish the light of the ghost but cannot put it out. The ghost is shown to be gentle and quiet but also firm when trying to explain his points and appears to be only in existance for a short while to speak to Scrooge.

The Ghost of Christmas Present

The Ghost of Christmas Present is described as Jolly and is considerably more cheerful than the previous ghost. This is shown by his welcoming and inviting attitude, however the ghost is also prophetic and honest even using Scrogges own words against him to prove a point in order to make scrooge question himself with questions that relate to what he has done.

The Ghost of Christmas Future

The Ghost of Chirstmas Future or of Christmas yet to come is completely silent when he appears but is described as a Dark and Ominous figure with a forbosing presence because of this he is also said to be frightening demanding and eerie whcih could be hinting to the future that is going to happen is Scrooge stays down the path he is heading.

Inspector Goole - Spirit of Socialism

"An impression of massiveness,solidify and purposefulness" This is how Inspector Goole is described and perfectly depicts his presence on the story and the characters. Inspecto Goole is a man in his fifties who seems to be an omnipinant figure who knows all about the mistakes that the family has made over the years and as such use them to punish them. There are many questions around Goole such as if he is a real person or if he is the voice of Priestly the author.

Themes

Theme 1- Christmas

The first theme is of the chirstmas spirit and hihglights Scrooges transformation from a mirser to a cheerful man, the theme of christmas is also explored by forgiveness and compassion and the importance of love and family.

Theme 2- Redemption

The next theme shown is redemption and is one of the key aspects shown by scrooge as he starts of disrespecting everyone and showing no kindness to becoming a kind person and trying to redeem himself for what he did .

Theme 3- Social Injustice

This is a big part of Scrooge before his change and is shown by his refusal to give money to the poor and shows the divide between people because of money or how they live.
